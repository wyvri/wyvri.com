poetry.

i hate ai.

i hate ai. it's everywhere,
maybe even in our hair.
now mother gaia isn't feeling very fair,
yet she can't let out a great wail.

our gait through fate
is falling off the trail.
now earth is feeling ailed,
its flair now tainted,
now stained and frail.

they said its for the best,
baited us with aided emails
and daisy praise;
they took claim of our brains,
used it for their own gains;
they saw our naivety,
and now we're our traitors.

now i shout out to no avail,
that them in their chairs
are all in a pair.
they've taken our acclaim,
reclaimed it as theirs;
put a curtain over our air,
as if they're not the corsairs.

this is not algebraic:
they're taking OUR paintwork,
proclaiming it theirs;
they're brainwashing us, draining us,
and yet there's no bewail!
i'm tired of being assailed,
of being a disdainful trainee.
i want to be an abstainer,
blissfully free.

now i find refrain
in the archaic.
